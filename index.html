Sketch Liquid Splashes with Neural Networks

Splashing liquid is one of the most fascinating phenomena in fluid dynamics. We propose to use deep generative models for liquid splashes modeling. We combine sketch based techniques with the data-driven approach. Sketching empowers the artists with the ability to draw the shapes very intuitively. Without simulation, our model can generate the output very quickly. So the artists is able to design and edit interactively to achieve a desired result.

Inexact Descent Methods for Elastic Parameter Optimization

Elastic parameter optimization is known to be computationally expensive. We propose to introduce the inexactness into descent methods, by iteratively solving a forward simulation step and a parameter update step in an inexact manner. Our method runs orders-of-magnitude faster than existing methods. We demonstrate the use of our inexact method in elastic material measurement, such as this synthetic skull model. And in interactive material design, as shown in this armadillo example, where the left arm is soft while the right arm is stiff, as expected.

Guowei Yan, Wei Li, Ruigang Yang and Huamin Wang. 2018. Inexact Descent Methods for Elastic Parameter Optimization. ACM Transactions on Graphics (SIGGRAPH Asia) PDF

Hyperelasticity: FEM Simulation

Creating believable and compelling character motions is the central challenge in animated movie productions. Manually rigging a character for each animation keyframe is tedious and time consuming. On the other hand, physics-based simulation excel at creating such realistic effects, such as the simulation of secondary motion, but are inherently difficult to control. Rig-Space Physics unifies keyframing and physical simulation by creating an underlying representation of the character based on an elastic deformable material and formulating the equations of motions in the subspace of deformations defined by an animator’s rig.

G.E.M: geometry modeling

G.E.M. is a lightweight 3D geometry modeling tool. It is very convenient for Curve Modeling, Basic Geometric Models Generation, Surface Modeling and Curve reconstruction. For curve modeling, you can generate Bezier curve, Cubic B-spline or Quadric B-spline. And then you can construct basic geometric models through operations like revolution, extrusion, sweeping and lofting. For Surface Modeling, besides the basic Bezier surface and B-spline surface, more general subdivision surfaces like Doo-Sabin surface, Catmull-Clark surface or Loop surface are also supported. 2D Curve reconstruction is implemented using Nearest Neighor Algorithm and Crust Algorithm.
